Macro-Major: Management and Entrepreneurship

Description:
Management and Entrepreneurship focuses on planning, organizing, leading, and managing businesses while also developing innovative ideas and creating new ventures. It combines business knowledge, leadership, strategy, and creativity to help individuals start, manage, and grow organizations in competitive markets.

Common Micro-Majors:
Business Administration, Entrepreneurship, Marketing, Finance, Accounting, International Business, Human Resource Management, Management of Information Systems

Key Topics:
Business Management, Entrepreneurship, Marketing, Finance, Business Strategy, Organizational Behavior, Leadership, Innovation, Business Planning, Operations Management

Common Skills:
Leadership, communication, problem-solving, creativity, decision-making, teamwork, adaptability, critical thinking, business understanding

Recommended High School Subjects:
Mathematics, English, Economics, Business Studies

Common Careers:
Entrepreneur, Business Manager, Project Manager, Marketing Manager, Financial Analyst, Operations Manager, Business Consultant, Startup Founder

Learning Style:
Students in this field often work on case studies, business simulations, group projects, presentations, and real-world business scenarios. The focus is on practical decision-making, leadership, and strategic thinking.

Related Macro-Majors:
Digital Technology, Economics and Finance, Social Sciences

Example Career Paths:
- Entrepreneurship → Startup Founder → Business Owner
- Marketing → Marketing Executive → Brand Manager
- Finance → Financial Analyst → Finance Manager
- Management → Project Manager → Operations Director
