Macro-Major: Public Service and Safety

Description:
Public Service and Safety focuses on serving communities, protecting people, maintaining public order, and supporting social well-being through government, law enforcement, emergency response, and public institutions. It combines responsibility, leadership, communication, ethics, and problem-solving to help create safe, organized, and supportive societies.

Common Micro-Majors:
Law, Public Administration, Political Science, International Relations, Criminology, Public Policy, Security Studies, Disaster Management, Social Work, Peace and Conflict Studies

Key Topics:
Public Administration, Law and Governance, Public Policy, Community Service, Criminal Justice, Emergency Management, Ethics, Human Rights, Leadership, Public Safety

Common Skills:
Communication, leadership, responsibility, critical thinking, problem-solving, teamwork, ethical awareness, decision-making, adaptability

Recommended High School Subjects:
English, History, Geography, Civics, Mathematics

Common Careers:
Public Officer, Policy Analyst, Social Worker, Police Officer, Legal Officer, Community Development Officer, Emergency Response Officer, Government Administrator, Security Officer

Learning Style:
Students in this field often work on case studies, policy discussions, community-based projects, teamwork activities, and practical problem-solving. The learning process focuses on leadership, ethics, communication, and applying knowledge to real social and public issues.

Related Macro-Majors:
Social Sciences, Law and Governance, Health and Community Services

Example Career Paths:
- Public Administration → Administrative Officer → Government Manager
- Law → Legal Assistant → Lawyer or Legal Officer
- Criminology and Safety → Security Officer → Public Safety Specialist
- Social Work → Community Support Officer → Social Development Specialist
