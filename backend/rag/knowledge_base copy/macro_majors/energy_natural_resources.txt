Macro-Major: Energy and Natural Resources

Description:
Energy and Natural Resources focuses on the study, management, and sustainable use of energy sources and natural resources such as water, minerals, forests, land, and fuel. It combines science, engineering, environmental understanding, and resource planning to support economic development while protecting natural systems.

Common Micro-Majors:
Energy Engineering, Environmental Science, Natural Resource Management, Renewable Energy, Petroleum Engineering, Mining Engineering, Water Resource Management, Forestry, Geology

Key Topics:
Energy Systems, Renewable Energy, Natural Resource Management, Environmental Protection, Water Resources, Geology, Sustainability, Resource Economics, Climate Change, Conservation

Common Skills:
Analytical thinking, problem-solving, observation, scientific understanding, environmental awareness, data interpretation, critical thinking, teamwork

Recommended High School Subjects:
Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Geography, English

Common Careers:
Energy Engineer, Environmental Officer, Natural Resource Manager, Renewable Energy Specialist, Geologist, Water Resource Officer, Sustainability Officer, Mining Engineer

Learning Style:
Students in this field often work on scientific analysis, field observation, technical problem-solving, environmental case studies, and practical projects related to energy production and resource management. The learning process usually combines theory, fieldwork, and applied research.

Related Macro-Majors:
Engineering, Environmental and Agricultural Sciences, Science and Research

Example Career Paths:
- Renewable Energy → Energy Technician → Renewable Energy Engineer
- Environmental Resources → Environmental Officer → Sustainability Manager
- Geology and Mining → Geologist → Mining Engineer
- Water Resources → Water Resource Assistant → Water Resource Specialist
